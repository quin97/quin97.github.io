About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Medical Biophysics at the University of Toronto, specializing in clinical research, multi-omic data integration, and translational bioinformatics. My research explores the complex interplay between host immune system, gut microbiota, and diabetes progression through computational approaches and innovative experimental design.

My research expertise includes:

  • Advanced machine learning and statistical modeling for complex biological datasets interpretation
  • Integration of multi-modal data (transcriptomic, immunophenotyping, microbiome) for predictive modeling
  • Translational approaches connecting biological mechanisms to observed phenotypes and clinical outcomes
  • Collaborative research across computational and experimental domains

I am passionate about bridging computational and experimental approaches to address pressing biomedical challenges, with emerging interests in neurocognitive aging and language processing. My research aims to transform our understanding of complex biological systems and develop predictive models that can inform personalized therapeutic strategies across diverse conditions, from metabolic disorders to age-related cognitive decline.

AI Projects

DataAssist: Automated Data Cleaning and Preparation

DataAssist implements a multi-stage machine learning pipeline for automated data preprocessing. The system employs isolation forests and DBSCAN with adaptive parameter tuning for multivariate outlier detection, MICE and KNN algorithms for missing value imputation, and applies Bayesian optimization to select optimal preprocessing strategies based on dataset characteristics.

CodeAssist: Neural Code Completion and Analysis

CodeAssist implements a modified transformer architecture with bidirectional attention and abstract syntax tree (AST) aware encoding for context-sensitive code completion. The model was trained on a 100GB+ corpus using a distributed PyTorch/Horovod pipeline on multi-GPU infrastructure.
